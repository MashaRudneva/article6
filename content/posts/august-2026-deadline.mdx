The full application date for most EU AI Act obligations is **2 August 2026**. For
providers of high-risk AI systems, this is not a soft deadline — it is the point at
which enforcement, and the associated fines of up to €35 million or 7% of global
turnover, become a real risk.

## What needs to be in place

If your AI system falls into the high-risk category under Annex III, you should have
the following in place before the deadline:

- **A risk management system** (Article 9) that runs across the entire lifecycle of
  the system, not just at launch.
- **Technical documentation** (Annex IV) sufficient to demonstrate conformity.
- **Data governance** practices covering training, validation, and testing data.
- **Human oversight** measures designed into the system.
- **A conformity assessment** completed and a declaration of conformity drawn up.

## Where most companies are behind

In my experience, the two areas where organisations are least prepared are the
**risk management system** and **post-market monitoring**. Both require ongoing
processes rather than one-off documentation, and both take time to embed.

If you have not yet classified your systems, that is the place to start. A structured
pre-assessment will tell you whether you are in scope and how much work stands between
you and the August deadline.
