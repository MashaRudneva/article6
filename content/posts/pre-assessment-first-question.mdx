Before you spend time and budget on a full conformity assessment, there is one
foundational question that determines everything that follows:

> **Is your AI system actually in scope of the EU AI Act — and if so, at what risk level?**

It sounds obvious. In practice, it is where most organisations either over-invest in
compliance they do not need, or under-invest in obligations they have not spotted.

## The four risk tiers

The Act sorts AI systems into four buckets:

- **Unacceptable risk** — prohibited outright (e.g. social scoring, certain biometric
  surveillance).
- **High risk** — permitted but heavily regulated (Annex III use cases, safety
  components).
- **Limited risk** — transparency obligations only (e.g. chatbots must disclose they
  are AI).
- **Minimal risk** — no specific obligations.

The gap between "high" and "limited" is enormous in terms of cost and effort. Getting
the classification right at the start is the single highest-leverage decision in the
whole process.

## Start small

A pre-assessment is deliberately lightweight. It answers the scope question, flags the
obligations that apply, and gives you a roadmap — without committing you to a full
assessment before you know whether you need one.
